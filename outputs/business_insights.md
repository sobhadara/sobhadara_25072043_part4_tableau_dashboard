## Task 2: Create Calculated Fields

In addition to the calculated fields explicitly required in Task 2, three additional calculated fields were created to enhance business analysis and provide deeper insights into customer behavior, profitability, and discount effectiveness.

### Calculated Fields

#### 1. Profit Margin

**Formula:**
```tableau
SUM([profit]) / SUM([sales])
```

**Purpose:**
Profit Margin measures the percentage of sales revenue retained as profit. This metric helps identify the most profitable categories, customer segments, and regions.

---

#### 2. Cost

**Formula:**
```tableau
[sales] - [profit]
```

**Purpose:**
Cost estimates the expense incurred to generate sales revenue and provides additional context for profitability analysis.

---

#### 3. Average Order Value

**Formula:**
```tableau
SUM([sales]) / COUNTD([order_id])
```

**Purpose:**
Average Order Value (AOV) measures the average revenue generated per order and helps evaluate customer purchasing behavior.

---

#### 4. Return Rate

**Formula:**
```tableau
SUM([return_flag]) / COUNT([order_id])
```

**Purpose:**
Return Rate measures the percentage of orders returned by customers and helps monitor product quality and customer satisfaction.

---

#### 5. Shipping Delay Bucket

**Formula:**
```tableau
IF [delivery_days] <= 2 THEN "Fast (0-2 Days)"
ELSEIF [delivery_days] <= 5 THEN "Standard (3-5 Days)"
ELSE "Delayed (6+ Days)"
END
```

**Purpose:**
This field categorizes shipping performance into meaningful groups for easier analysis of delivery efficiency.

---

### Additional Calculated Fields

#### 6. Discount Percentage

**Formula:**
```tableau
[discount] * 100
```

**Purpose:**
Converts discount values into percentage format, making it easier to analyze the relationship between discounts and profitability.

---

#### 7. Profit per Order

**Formula:**
```tableau
SUM([profit]) / COUNTD([order_id])
```

**Purpose:**
Profit per Order measures the average profit generated from each order and provides insight into transaction-level profitability.

---

#### 8. Sales per Customer

**Formula:**
```tableau
SUM([sales]) / COUNTD([customer_id])
```

**Purpose:**
Sales per Customer measures the average revenue generated by each unique customer and helps evaluate customer value.

---

## Business Value of the Calculated Fields

The calculated fields enhance the dashboard by providing meaningful business metrics beyond the raw transactional data. Profit Margin and Profit per Order help assess financial performance and profitability. Average Order Value and Sales per Customer provide insight into customer purchasing behavior and customer value. Return Rate and Shipping Delay Bucket support operational monitoring by highlighting return patterns and shipping efficiency. Cost and Discount Percentage help explain profitability drivers and identify opportunities to improve margins.

Together, these calculated fields support leadership decision-making by enabling comprehensive analysis of sales performance, profitability, customer segments, category performance, shipping performance, discount impact, and return patterns.

# Task 8: Business Insights

## Insight 1: Sales Trend

**Observation:**  
Monthly sales fluctuate throughout the year, with noticeable peaks and dips.

**Data Evidence:**  
Sales are highest in February and October (around ₹20M) and lowest in April (around ₹15M).

**Business Interpretation:**  
Sales appear to be seasonal, indicating stronger customer demand during specific periods.

**Recommended Action:**  
Investigate the reasons behind the April decline and plan promotional campaigns before low-performing months.

---

## Insight 2: Regional Performance

**Observation:**  
The South region generates the highest sales among all regions.

**Data Evidence:**  
South records approximately ₹65M in sales, followed by North at ₹55M, while East and West contribute about ₹49M each.

**Business Interpretation:**  
The South region is the strongest market and contributes significantly to overall revenue.

**Recommended Action:**  
Maintain strong performance in the South while identifying opportunities to improve sales in the East and West regions.

---

## Insight 3: Category and Sub-Category Profitability

**Observation:**  
Technology products generate the highest profits.

**Data Evidence:**  
Sub-categories such as Copiers, Accessories, Phones, and Machines contribute the largest profit values.

**Business Interpretation:**  
Technology is the most profitable product category and has strong customer demand.

**Recommended Action:**  
Focus marketing efforts and inventory planning on high-profit Technology products while reviewing the performance of lower-profit categories.

---

## Insight 4: Customer Segment Behavior

**Observation:**  
The Home Office segment generates the highest sales and profit.

**Data Evidence:**  
Home Office records approximately ₹75M in sales and around ₹12M in profit, outperforming Consumer and Corporate segments.

**Business Interpretation:**  
The Home Office segment is the most valuable customer segment for the business.

**Recommended Action:**  
Develop targeted promotions and loyalty programs to further increase engagement within the Home Office segment.

---

## Insight 5: Discount Impact

**Observation:**  
Higher discount percentages are generally associated with lower profit values.

**Data Evidence:**  
The Discount vs Profit scatter plot shows profits reducing as discount percentages increase.

**Business Interpretation:**  
Large discounts negatively affect profitability even if they help increase sales volume.

**Recommended Action:**  
Review the discount policy and avoid excessive discounts on already low-margin products.

---

## Insight 6: Shipping and Delivery Performance

**Observation:**  
Shipping performance varies across different shipping modes.

**Data Evidence:**  
The Shipping Performance View compares average delivery days for each shipping mode.

**Business Interpretation:**  
Some shipping methods take longer than others, which may affect customer satisfaction.

**Recommended Action:**  
Investigate slower shipping methods and improve logistics to reduce delivery time.

---

## Insight 7: Return Pattern

**Observation:**  
Return rates differ across customer segments and product categories.

**Data Evidence:**  
The Return Analysis View and dashboard KPI show an overall return rate of approximately 4.55%.

**Business Interpretation:**  
Returns represent a cost to the business and may indicate product quality or customer expectation issues.

**Recommended Action:**  
Analyze products and customer segments with higher return rates to identify the root causes and reduce returns.
